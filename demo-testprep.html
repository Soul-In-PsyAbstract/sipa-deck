Channel Assessment

What TPI already has right

The core content model is structurally sound: real product testing, affiliate-aligned review content, high-intent search traffic. The "we actually buy the course" positioning is a genuine differentiator that most review channels can't match. This is a monetization-ready channel. The growth work is about unlocking the ceiling, not rebuilding the floor.

The opportunity is in three specific areas where mechanics, not effort, are limiting performance.

Growth Opportunities

Where the ceiling is

▲
Thumbnail CTR on comparison videos
Comparison content ("Kaplan vs Princeton Review") carries the highest purchase intent on the channel. Generic thumbnails with product logos underperform against face-forward verdict thumbnails. This is the highest-leverage single change.
Priority 1
▲
Title formula for non-comparison content
Review titles often lead with the product name. Curiosity-gap titles that lead with the outcome ("Why 80% of GRE students overpay for prep") consistently outperform product-first titles in education verticals.
Priority 2
▲
Shorts as decision-stage content
Most test prep shorts are repurposed long-form clips. Shorts built specifically around micro-verdicts ("The one reason I'd pick Magoosh over Kaplan") perform 3–5× better as standalone content and feed long-form watch time.
Priority 3
